Csound

Since initially learning about wave interaction and overtones and building Overtone Lab, I've begun doing research with Csound. Csound is a programming language that digitally generates sound. It can be used to create synthesizers and physical models of real instruments, given knowledge of the mechanics of waves and the instrument's physical properties. Cabbage Audio takes Csound code and turns it into VST plugins that can be exported and used with MIDI inside any Digital Audio Workstation (DAW). My current work with Dr. Richard Boulanger of Berklee College of Music focuses on porting and modifying instruments from the Csound Collection into Cabbage and turning them into DAW plugins.
